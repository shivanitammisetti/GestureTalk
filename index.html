Help

Quick help for using GestureTalk

Why is my hand not being detected? +
Make sure your hand is clearly visible in front of the camera. Place your hand in the center of the frame and avoid moving too fast. Good lighting also improves detection accuracy.
How should I position my hand? +
Keep your palm facing the camera and ensure your entire hand is visible. Try keeping a small distance between your hand and the camera so the system can clearly detect your fingers.
What if the wrong letter appears? +
Hold the gesture steady for a moment before changing to the next sign. Sudden movement may reduce recognition accuracy.
Do I need to know sign language to use this? +
Yes. GestureTalk recognizes standard hand signs. If you are new to sign language, you can refer to the hand signs guide below.

About

Understanding GestureTalk

GestureTalk

Bridging Sign Language and Spoken Communication

v1.0.0

GestureTalk is an assistive communication system designed to help people who use sign language communicate easily with others. Using a live camera feed, the system detects hand gestures in real time and converts them into meaningful text and speech.

Users simply perform sign language gestures in front of the camera. The system recognizes the gesture, converts it into text, forms words or sentences, and can speak the message aloud using text-to-speech. This allows individuals who rely on sign language to interact more naturally with people who may not understand it.

GestureTalk aims to reduce communication barriers and promote accessibility by turning hand gestures into spoken language instantly, making everyday conversations more inclusive and accessible.
